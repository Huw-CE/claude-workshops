Claude isn't one thing. There are three models and they're not interchangeable. Using Opus to write a subject line is like hiring a senior strategist to book a taxi. Here's the quick guide — and the rule for when to switch.

The Three Models

Know which one you're talking to.

Everyday workhorse

Claude Sonnet

Use it for

Fast, capable, and the right choice for 90% of what you do. Drafting copy, reviewing briefs, answering questions, running skills. If you're not sure which to use, use this one. It's on by default for a reason.

Heavy lifting

Claude Opus

Use it for

Slower and more expensive, but significantly smarter on complex tasks. Use it for long document analysis, nuanced strategic thinking, or anything where the quality of reasoning actually matters — not just the speed of output.

Speed over depth

Claude Haiku

Use it for

The fastest and cheapest. Good for quick lookups, simple reformatting, or when you're burning through tokens and need to conserve. Not for creative or strategic work — you'll notice the quality gap immediately.

Task Recommended model Why
Writing a tagline or copy draft Sonnet Fast, creative, more than adequate for short-form output
Auditing a 40-page strategy deck Opus Longer reasoning chain, better at holding large context
Reformatting a spreadsheet or list Haiku No reasoning required — pure transformation task
Running a skills workflow Sonnet Good balance of capability and speed for structured tasks
Complex strategic recommendation Opus You want the best reasoning, not the fastest answer
Quick lookup or factual check Haiku Doesn't need depth — save tokens for heavier work
Managing Context

Your session gets dumber
as it gets longer.

Claude has a context window — the amount of conversation it can "see" at once. As sessions grow, quality degrades. Here's how to manage it.

  • Long sessions degrade in quality as context fills. Start a new Project when switching tasks — don't let one conversation carry everything.
  • Use the Caveman skill mid-conversation to compress context before it bloats. It strips filler and halves the token count without losing substance.
  • Summarise long threads manually — paste your summary and say "Continue from here." Claude will pick up without needing to re-read the whole thread.
  • Projects maintain memory across sessions. Use them for every ongoing client. The alternative is starting from scratch every time you open Claude.
